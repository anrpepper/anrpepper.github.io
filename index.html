Study of phase polyphenism emergence and associated risks

  Locust species alternatively display harmless solitarious and gregarious phenotypes. The latter create migrating bands and swarms that are devastating for all types of agriculture. This plasticity is called phase polyphenism.

  The first objective of PEPPER will be to theoretically explore the trade-offs and environmental conditions leading to phase polyphenism emergence. Identifying the regimes of environmental variation of resources favorable to phase polyphenism evolution may help in identifying conditions of outbreaks.

  The second objective of PEPPER will be to develop a forecasting model of locust population dynamics and validate it using field data from locust observations.

  Demogenetic agent-based model should be used in the first objective. The second objective will also benefit from integrating the biological knowledge on the Desert locust within a mechanistic agent-based model that will consider remote-sensing imagery to represent environmental drivers. Pattern-oriented modelling will be applied to both objectives to select the processes to consider in the models and adjust them to real-world data.

  Cyril Piou is an ecological modeler with long experience on agent-based models. He works on locust ecology since 2010.

Beginning and duration: February 2019 - 42 months
